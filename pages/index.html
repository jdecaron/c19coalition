Leadership response to the urgent shortages of Personal Protective Equipment

Many people across the nation are stepping up to solve the crisis that is afflicting our heroic frontline health workers and first responders. We are distributors and hospitals, manufacturers and doctors, teams matchmaking between hospitals and suppliers, 3D printers, grassroots efforts to find unused or novel sources of supply, and importers from global supply chains.Our members are committed to leading the way out of the crisis. This site is designed to bring awareness to the efforts underway and to help donors, volunteers, policymakers and providers connect to the right initiative.Did we miss something? Please let us know. The Need

As hospitals struggle to find safe and reliable supplies, thousands of companies, organizations, and individuals are stepping up to get health care workers the personal protective equipment and lifesaving medical devices they need. But no single organization can do this alone. In fact, the proliferation of organizations in this space has made it incredibly challenging to navigate.

National PPE Coalition is working on connecting everybody - state governments, corporations, manufacturers, non profits, and individuals - to help them create a new supply chain in real time. We’re doing that by sending real time updates about what everybody is doing, connecting members to each other, and directing people in need to organizations that can help.

The Coalition

Our members are working on every part of the PPE problem - supply, demand, data tracking, logistics, fulfillment, funding, and more. From single individuals raising donations or sewing masks to multinational corporations producing badly needed ventilators and respirators, everybody plays a part.

The coalition is an open organization, and our goal is to get everybody working on these critical initiatives listed here in order to encourage communication and coordination.

Connecting need to supply

As hospitals struggle to find safe and reliable equipment, several organizations have come together to shake up the supply chain, improve logistics, and help save lives. Several coalition members are acting as clearinghouses for medical equipment by matching buyers to suppliers, tracking urgent needs and helping with logistics to ensure that PPE gets to where it is needed most. They’ve taken in thousands of institution requests so far and started making matches with suppliers.

Project N95

Project N95 is focused on delivering medical equipment into the hands of healthcare providers as quickly as possibly by serving as the nation's medical clearinghouse for hospital and healthcare requests and suppliers who are able to provide medical equipment, including ventilators. They’re working with FEMA, governments, and hospitals and healthcare networks to determine where demand is and where it is most urgent.

GetUsPPE

Get Us PPE is a national, centralized platform that leverages a large network of partners and volunteers to: facilitate safe and efficient delivery of supplies to local hospitals, advocate for immediate action to expedite the manufacture of medical PPE nationwide and unite the maker community to validate and share PPE designs that can be printed and assembled locally. It is a collaboration between physicians and medical researchers on the frontlines of the COVID-19 pandemic, engineers, scientists and programmers.

PPE Link

PPE Link is working to collect and connect donations of standard PPE (not homemade) from local communities of academic and industry STEM labs as well as any other institutions or companies that use PPE in their operations to help provide a short-term stopgap by donating their local hospitals with supplies until national efforts are able to compensate.

Masks For Docs

Masks for Docs is focused exclusively on providing protective masks to health care workers. They are collecting masks and have volunteers making and printing masks, and are building a resource to match supplies with hospitals in need and distribute them.

Operation Masks

Operation Masks has partnered with logistics experts to get masks, gloves and other PPE from payment to factory procurement to shipping to ground delivery to hospitals.

Help Hospitals

Help Hospitals is working to catalog hospitals and their supply needs in order to connect with the appropriate donations of PPE.

Harbor Freight

Harbor Freight is shipping more than 44 million pairs of nitrile gloves and hundreds of thousands of masks and face shields to hospitals in over 1,000 communities that requested them directly.

So You Want To Help with PPE

A list of resources for helping with the shortage of PPE, including places to donate and volunteer, plans and specs for sewing, 3D printing and donating masks, ways to request masks and face shields, answers to frequently asked questions about making masks and other ways to help both regionally and nationally

Find the Masks

Find the Masks has catalogued nationwide donation locations for PPE including surgical mask & N95 Masks, face shields, medical booties, safety goggles, gloves, kleenex, hand sanitizer, medical overalls, gowns, and advanced respirators (PAPR/CAPR/etc.). Filter by location or type of PPE.

Mask Match

Mask Match is a peer-to-peer matching site for direct shipping between those who have masks to donate and those who are in need.

Get Them PPE

Get Them PEE is a crowdsourcing site built to help identify hospitals and other organizations that are severely undersupplied and in need of donations. Use the map to find out what items local hospitals are in need of and get contact info for each hospital to coordinate donations. There is also a separate form to submit hospital needs for inclusion.

Worldwide Supply Chain Federation

The Worldwide Supply Chain Federation is using its collective resources as a grassroots supply chain to source the most critical materials. It’s working with a number of government and private organizations to get everything where it is most needed, and to minimize random inquiries these groups need to vet and screen through.

Mask Brigade

The goal of each Mask Brigade’s is to help local residents and small businesses donate their N95 masks to the local healthcare community. Volunteers will pick up donated N95 masks as well as any other PPE and take them to local hospitals. They also offer information on methods for direct donation.

Sourcing new supply

Demand is surging, and supply chains aren’t able to keep up. To fill the gap, these coalition members are working around the clock to produce new sources of equipment. Groups of entrepreneurs in technology, healthcare and logistics are securing overseas and domestic production and shipment of all types of PPE supplies. Other coalition members are helping fund domestic production and even 3-D printing badly needed equipment and parts.

The Mask Pros

The Mask Pros provides free resources for making masks including sewing patterns, DIY instructional videos, and downloadable files for 3D-printed masks. It also includes general information about surgical masks and N95 respirator masks

FreeSewing

FreeSewing is an open source platform for made-to-measure sewing patterns. They’re providing several different facemask patterns on PDF and an instructional video that shows how to make them.

Rosie Sews

Rosie Sews is providing patterns for sewing face masks as well as coordinating the efforts of seamstresses, makers and manufacturers to make masks and other supplies for use by medical professionals, at-risk community members and essential workers. They’re also working on converting maker spaces across the nation into supply production sites.

Nineteenth Amendment (N.A. bld): Make A Mask

Make A Mask is providing a tech kit with a free fabric face mask template, complete with digital pattern, suggested materials, and sourcing info for making masks at home. It’s also looking to partner with manufacturers to make masks, accepting donations and distributing masks.

Million Mask Challenge

Million Mask Challenge is providing protective face mask patterns, hygiene protocol and mailing instructions for finished masks. They’re also offering an N95 trade in program so non-medical professionals who have N95's can trade them in for the masks created from the organization’s specs, which they will then send to the doctors.

Operation We Can Sew It!

Operation We Can Sew It! is providing a cloth mask design approved by Saint Joseph Hospital in Denver, CO and SCL Health facilities. All masks made to the specifications of our pattern will be accepted at these facilities and may be elsewhere but this should be approved beforehand.

Massachusetts Life Sciences Emergency Supply Hub

Massachusetts Life Sciences Emergency Supply Hub is helping coordinate efforts to bring additional supplies and resources to Massachusetts healthcare institutions. They’re asking life sciences and healthcare organizations to consider how they may be able to help, specifically by donating lab, testing, and diagnostics supplies, personal protection equipment (PPE), and medical and scientific expertise needed to combat COVID-19.

Massachusetts COVID-19 Manufacturing Capacity Index

As part of a coordinated response between academic, industry, and government, the Massachusetts COVID-19 Manufacturing Capacity Index team is indexing the Commonwealth's manufacturing capacity to produce critically-needed components. Organizations NOT located within the State of MA can also complete the form, and the information will be used for coordinating efforts between other state and federal initiatives.

Paying for everything

Foundations and nonprofits are stepping up to get healthcare workers the equipment they need to fight COVID-19 by floating capital and raising funds from individual donors. Several coalition members are raising significant sums to purchase large supplies of PPE and distribute them to hospital systems that are most in need. Some of our individual members are running GoFundMe campaigns to aggregate small donors and make a big difference.

Providing data

In a public health emergency like this, people need accurate information to protect themselves and their families. Coalition members are helping you stay safe by slowing the spread of the virus and misinformation at the same time. These groups are doing everything from providing guidelines for reducing the burden on the health care system to helping folks understand social distancing to showing individuals the proper uses of protective equipment.
